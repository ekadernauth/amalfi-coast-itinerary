July 3 & July 8 · 2 Nights

Naples — Napoli

One of Italy's great raw, chaotic, magnificent cities. The birthplace of pizza, home to world-class Roman artifacts, and full of neighborhoods that reward slow, curious walking.

⚠️ Book ahead: The Cappella Sansevero and Underground Naples sell out weeks in advance in July. Book these immediately after your hotels.
Must-Book in Advance
🏺
Naples National Archaeological Museum (MANN)
World-class Pregnancy-friendly
📍 Piazza Museo Nazionale, 19
The finest collection of Roman artifacts in the world — all from Pompeii and Herculaneum. The mosaics, bronzes, and Secret Cabinet are genuinely extraordinary. Fully climate-controlled — a perfect midday refuge from July heat. Allow 2–3 hours minimum.
💡 Go first thing at 9am before tour groups. Closed Tuesdays.
Official Site → Reviews → €15 · Daily 9am–7:30pm · Closed Tue
🎭
Cappella Sansevero — The Veiled Christ
Unmissable Book 3–4 weeks ahead
📍 Via Francesco De Sanctis, 19
A marble sculpture — Christ beneath a translucent veil carved from one block in 1753 — so perfect visitors reach out expecting fabric. No photos. Timed entry sells out in July. One of the most astonishing things you will ever see.
💡 30 minutes inside is enough. Book your time slot at museosansevero.it immediately.
Book Tickets → €8 · Wed–Mon 9am–7pm · No photos
🕯️
Napoli Sotterranea — Underground Naples
Book ahead
📍 Piazza San Gaetano, 68
250 miles of tunnels beneath the city carved over 2,400 years. Guided tours go 40 meters underground through Greek aqueducts and WWII shelters, carrying candles through passages so narrow shoulders scrape the walls. 80-minute tours.
💡 Pregnancy note: Narrow passages and steps — manageable in 2nd trimester but confirm with the venue in advance.
Book Tickets → €10 · Tours daily at 10, 12, 2, 4, 6pm
Free or Low Cost
🌊
Castel dell'Ovo & Lungomare
Free Pregnancy-friendly
📍 Via Eldorado, 3 · Santa Lucia waterfront
Naples' oldest castle jutting into the bay with views of Vesuvius and Capri. Free to enter. The Lungomare Caracciolo promenade is one of the most beautiful evening walks in Italy — locals jog, families stroll. Completely flat.
💡 Best at golden hour (7–8pm in July) when Vesuvius turns pink. Perfect first evening walk.
Reviews → Free · Daily 9am–7:30pm
⛪
Santa Chiara Cloisters
Pregnancy-friendly
📍 Via Santa Chiara, 49c · Spaccanapoli
A 14th-century courtyard lined with vivid 18th-century majolica tiles — an extraordinary oasis of silence in the middle of Naples' noise. Unlike anything else in Italy. A perfect 30-minute escape from the heat and chaos outside.
💡 Enter from the side entrance on the left — the cloister is separate from the church.
Official Site → €6 · Mon–Sat 9:30am–5:30pm · Sun 10am–2:30pm
👑
Piazza del Plebiscito & Royal Palace
Piazza free
📍 Piazza del Plebiscito, 1
The grandest piazza in Naples, flanked by the Royal Palace and the imposing San Francesco di Paola church. The palace has grand staircases, velvet rooms, ceiling frescoes, and a miniature royal theatre. The piazza itself is free and one of the best places to sit with an espresso.
💡 Free palace entry on the first Sunday of each month.
Royal Palace → Piazza free · Palace €10 · Thu–Tue 9am–8pm
🎨
Catacombs of San Gennaro
Less crowded
📍 Via Capodimonte, 13 · Rione Sanità
Sweeping two-story subterranean basilicas with 2nd-century Christian frescoes — more beautiful and less claustrophobic than Underground Naples. Located in the authentic Rione Sanità neighborhood. Worth the short taxi ride. Guided tours only.
💡 Walk down to Via San Gregorio Armeno after — the nativity craft street is open year-round.
Book Tickets → €9 · Tours daily 10am–5pm
Naples' neighborhoods are as much of an attraction as its monuments. The real city lives in these streets.
🛵
Spaccanapoli & Via dei Tribunali
Start here Free
📍 Runs east-west through the Historic Center · UNESCO
The street that literally "splits Naples" — a perfectly straight line along the ancient Greek road grid. Motorcycles speed past, shopkeepers argue, walls covered in religious art and graffiti. Via dei Tribunali running parallel is the pizza and food street. More Neapolitan life per meter than anywhere else in the city.
💡 Walk east from Piazza del Gesù Nuovo — the street gets progressively more local and less touristy.
View on Map → Free · Best 8–11am · Allow 1–2 hours

Quartieri Spagnoli — Spanish Quarter
Local favorite Free
📍 West of Via Toledo
16th-century alleyways — washing lines overhead, street shrines on every corner, small bars and trattorias with no tourist menus. Home to the famous Maradona murals. This is where locals eat lunch on a Tuesday. Genuinely authentic.
💡 Look for the Maradona mural on Via Emanuele De Deo — a surprisingly moving piece of street art.

The long seafront promenade running from Santa Lucia west toward the Posillipo hills. Closed to traffic evenings and weekends. Completely flat. The stretch near Castel dell'Ovo at sunset is extraordinary. Perfect first-evening walk.
💡 3km of flat walking. Pick up a gelato from a kiosk near Castel dell'Ovo.

Where locals go Free
📍 Northern edge of Historic Center
Outdoor café terraces built around ancient Greek city walls — where young Neapolitans come for aperitivo. Locals specifically call this out as "the real Naples." Best at 6–8pm as the city cools down.
💡 Order an Aperol Spritz or Campari Soda and people-watch. Perfect pre-dinner ritual.
View on Map → Free · Best 6–9pm

🥙 Essential Naples Street Food

  • Pizza a portafoglio — pizza folded into four, eaten standing on Via dei Tribunali. €1.50–2. How locals eat pizza at lunch.
  • Sfogliatella — lobster-tail pastry of shatteringly crisp layers filled with hot citrus ricotta cream. Buy warm from a bakery. €1.50–2.
  • Frittatina di pasta — deep-fried pasta cake with béchamel and ragù inside. €2 from street windows. Sounds weird, tastes extraordinary.
  • Cuoppo di mare — a paper cone of mixed fried seafood. €3–5 near the waterfront.
  • Pizza fritta — fried pizza, born in the postwar years. €2.50. Look for it in the Spanish Quarter.

L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele
Legendary
📍 Via Cesare Sersale, 1 · Est. 1870 · Walk-in only
The most famous pizzeria in the world. Two pizzas only: Margherita and Marinara. No reservations, no substitutions, €5–7. Known from Eat Pray Love but beloved by Neapolitans for 150 years. 🍽 Dining Like a Local in Naples

  • Dinner starts at 8:30–9pm. Tip by rounding up or leaving €1–2 — the American 20% is not expected.
  • Tap water is rarely served — bottled still or sparkling is the norm.
  • Order a caffè sospeso (suspended coffee) at any bar — you pay for two, the second goes to whoever needs one. A Naples tradition of solidarity.
Naples takes coffee more seriously than anywhere else in Italy. Locals drink standing at the bar, pay €1, and leave. The whole ritual takes under 3 minutes.

Procida — Italy's Hidden Gem

Tiny, colorful, genuine. Italy's 2022 Capital of Culture. Only 4 square kilometers — you can walk across it. The most unspoiled island in the Bay of Naples and a perfect full day trip.

Procida is less a cultural destination and more a retreat where you slow down and enjoy timeless Mediterranean life. The whole charm is in simply being there.
🏘️
Marina Corricella
Don't miss
📍 South side of the island · No cars
The most beautiful spot on Procida — a car-free fishing village of pastel houses stacked above a small harbor where fishermen still weave their nets. Featured in The Talented Mr. Ripley and Dolce & Gabbana campaigns. Give yourself at least 2 hours here.
💡 Descend from above for the iconic stacked-houses view before going down to the waterfront.
Reviews → Free · Best in morning light
🏰
Terra Murata — Historic Hilltop
Best views
📍 Highest point of the island
The ancient historic center crowned by the 11th-century Abbazia di San Michele. Walk through narrow streets to the hilltop — views over the Gulf of Naples are extraordinary. Visit the abbey and nearby Palazzo D'Avalos (former prison). Take the C2 bus or walk up.
💡 Go early morning before the heat builds. The walk up rewards you with increasingly good views.
Reviews → Free to walk · Abbey free · D'Avalos tour €12
🏖️
Spiaggia Chiaiolella
Best swimming
📍 Western side of the island
The best and most accessible beach on Procida — long sandy stretch with calm, clear water overlooking Ischia. Far less crowded than anything on Capri. Mix of free public beach and equipped sunbed areas. Perfect for a long afternoon swim.
💡 The western end near Chiaiolella town is most scenic and has the best waterside restaurants.
Reviews → Free public beach · Sunbeds for hire
🚲
Rent an E-Bike & Explore
Best way around
📍 Rental shops near Marina Grande port
The best way to explore Procida in a day — the island is tiny but hilly, and e-bikes make it effortless. Around €30 for the day. Lap the entire island multiple times and stop wherever catches your eye. No cars allowed April–October so roads are peaceful.
💡 Pregnancy note — e-bikes are smooth and manageable in the second trimester. The electric assist removes almost all effort on hills.
Find Rentals → ~€30/day · Available at Marina Grande

🍋 Procida's Signature Flavors

  • Insalata di limoni — fresh local lemon slices with garlic, olive oil, red pepper, mint, and salt. The lemons here are less acidic and naturally sweeter. Try it everywhere.
  • Spaghetti con le canocchie — pasta with locally caught mantis shrimp. A Procida specialty you won't easily find elsewhere.
  • Spaghetti ai ricci di mare — pasta with sea urchin. Intensely rich and briny — the taste of the island in a bowl.
  • Lingua di bue — the local pastry ("ox tongue") unique to Procida. Get one at any bar with your morning coffee.
  • Lemon granita — made with Procida's famous lemons. Felice Mare is the most celebrated granita spot on the island.

Everything you need to know for a perfect Procida day trip from Naples.

🚢 Ferry Logistics

  • Depart from: Molo Beverello port, Naples (the waterfront port — not the train station)
  • Journey time: ~40 minutes by hydrofoil (aliscafo)
  • Frequency: Several departures per morning in July — take the 8:00–9:00am boat
  • Return: Last ferries back to Naples around 7–8pm
  • Operators: Caremar and SNAV run this route — tickets at port or online
  • Important: No private cars allowed on the island from April–October

🤰 For Your Pregnant Traveler

  • E-bikes are the best transport option — gentle, no effort, you control the pace.
  • Chiaiolella beach is the most accessible for swimming — flat and calm water.
  • Most Corricella restaurants have outdoor terraces — stop for lunch early (12:30pm) to avoid midday heat.
  • All food on Procida is cooked seafood — nothing to avoid. The lemon salad is pregnancy-safe.
  • Shade is limited in Terra Murata — go early morning before 10am.

📅 Suggested Day Flow

  • 8:30am — Ferry from Naples (Molo Beverello)
  • 9:15am — Arrive Marina Grande, rent e-bikes, ride up to Terra Murata for morning views
  • 10:30am — Descend to Marina Corricella — wander the lanes and harbourfront
  • 12:30pm — Long seafood lunch at Il Pescatore or Da Girone
  • 2:30pm — Bike across to Chiaiolella beach for afternoon swim
  • 5:00pm — Aperitivo at a harbourside bar watching the sunset over Ischia
  • 6:30pm — Ferry back to Naples
🆘 Key Emergency Numbers: 112 (all emergencies / ambulance) · 118 (medical ambulance) · 115 (fire) · 1530 (coast guard / sea emergencies). Anyone can access Italian public emergency care regardless of nationality or documentation.

📍 Day Trip Note

  • Procida is a day trip — you return to Palazzo Caracciolo, Naples in the evening. There is a small medical post on the island. For anything serious, 118 will coordinate evacuation to the mainland.

🤰 Pregnancy Note on Procida

  • The island medical post can handle initial assessment and call for evacuation if needed.
  • For any pregnancy-related concern, call 118 immediately — they will determine if sea or air evacuation is needed.

Sorrento — Your Home Base

A genuine Italian town perched on cliffs above the Bay of Naples. Flat, walkable, excellent food, and the most practical base on the entire coast. Ferry connections to Capri, Positano, and Naples all within easy reach.

Sorrento is a city for relaxing and enjoying southern Italian hospitality. The pleasure is in slow strolling, the views, and the food. Use it as a launchpad and let it be a restorative base between adventures.
🌅
Villa Comunale Park & Bay Views
Free Pregnancy-friendly
📍 Near the clifftop, central Sorrento
A small park perched high above the sea with panoramic views of the Bay of Naples and Mount Vesuvius. On a clear day you can see Capri, Ischia, and Procida. One of the most romantic sunset spots in Italy. The Sorrento Lift to the ferry port is right next door.
💡 Visit at sunset — the Vesuvius silhouette against a pink sky is one of the great free views on the coast.
View on Map → Free · Best 7–8pm in July
🛍️
Piazza Tasso & Corso Italia
Free
📍 Heart of Sorrento
Piazza Tasso is the animated main square — the place to sit with an Aperol Spritz at Fauno Bar and people-watch. Corso Italia is the main shopping street where locals do their evening passeggiata. Lined with ceramic shops, limoncello stores, leather goods, and good cafés.
💡 The Via San Cesareo lanes branching off Piazza Tasso are where locals shop for fruit and vegetables — charming and authentic.
View on Map → Free · Best in the evening
🏺
Vallone dei Mulini — Valley of the Mills
Free to view
📍 Behind Piazza Tasso · visible from the railing
A hidden overgrown gorge right in the center of Sorrento — ancient ruins of a water-powered mill swallowed by lush vegetation, visible from the railing just a few meters from Piazza Tasso. One of the most surreal unexpected sights in the town. Takes 5 minutes and is completely free.
💡 Look for the railings on Viale Enrico Caruso, just off Piazza Tasso. Takes 5 minutes.
View on Map → Free · 5-minute stop · Always open
⛪
Cloister of San Francesco
Free Pregnancy-friendly
📍 Via San Francesco · Near Villa Comunale
A Medieval cloister from the 1300s — peaceful, shaded, and beautiful. Free to enter. The arched courtyard with its interlocking Arab-Sicilian and Gothic arches is a quiet respite from the heat of the town. Often used as a venue for the Sorrento classical music festival in July.
💡 Right next to Villa Comunale — combine both into a 30-minute morning or late-afternoon walk.
View on Map → Free · Morning hours

Marina Grande — The Fishing Village
Hidden in plain sight

🚢 Getting Around the Coast from Sorrento

  • Sorrento → Positano ferry: 35–40 min · Calm morning sea · Book in advance
  • Positano → Amalfi ferry: 30 min · Multiple daily departures · Scenic coastal route
  • Amalfi → Ravello: SITA bus (20–30 min, €1.50) or taxi (€15–20) up the mountain
  • COSTIERASITA 24-hr ticket: €10 from any tabacchi — unlimited bus travel between all Amalfi Coast towns
  • Warning: July SITA buses are extremely crowded. Ferries are always the better option between coastal towns.
  • Taxi tips: Negotiate fare before getting in. Positano to Amalfi ~€60–80. Amalfi to Ravello ~€15.

🤰 Amalfi Coast Notes for a Pregnant Traveler

  • Positano stairs: The town is essentially all stairs. Take them slowly, wear flat sandals with grip.
  • Avoid the bus: The winding Amalfi Drive by bus is famously motion-sickness-inducing. Ferries between towns are much gentler.
  • Beach clubs: Pre-booking sunbeds means you arrive to shade and a lounger — no carrying things down stairs in the heat.
  • Ravello is easier: More flat than Positano, genuinely peaceful, with shaded garden paths.
  • Hydration: Carry water everywhere. The July heat on the coast is extreme — 35°C+ is common.
  • Morning priority: Do active things before noon. Rest in shade or at the beach midday.
🆘 Key Emergency Numbers: 112 (all emergencies / ambulance) · 118 (medical ambulance) · 115 (fire) · 1530 (coast guard / sea emergencies). Anyone can access Italian public emergency care regardless of nationality or documentation.

📍 Most Remote Day — No Hospital in Positano or Ravello

  • Positano, Ravello, and Amalfi have no full hospitals. The nearest pronto soccorso is near Amalfi (~45 min from Positano by winding road). The ferry back to Sorrento (~35 min) is often the more practical and gentler route to your base hospital.

🤰 Pregnancy — Critical Notes for Positano Day

  • Positano has no hospital. The nearest facility is ~45 min by winding road (serious motion sickness risk). The ferry to Sorrento (~35 min) with its hospital is often safer and more practical.
  • Carry your OB documentation and week of pregnancy written in Italian: "Sono alla settimana X di gravidanza" — on a card in your bag.
  • If anything feels wrong: Call 118 immediately. A sea ambulance can reach coastal beach clubs. Do not try to self-transport on the winding coastal road.
  • Stay very well hydrated — July heat on the coast is intense and dehydration can cause worrying symptoms.
  • Plan an earlier return if you feel tired or unwell at any point during the day. The ferry schedule allows for flexible timing.

📋 Key Phrases & Emergency Protocol

  • "Chiami un'ambulanza" — Call an ambulance
  • "Sono incinta alla settimana [X]" — I am [X] weeks pregnant
  • "Ho dolore addominale / crampi" — I have abdominal pain / cramps
  • "Ho bisogno di aiuto urgente" — I need urgent help
  • Coast Guard 1530 — for emergencies at sea or at coastal beach clubs. They coordinate sea ambulance evacuation.
  • Ask your restaurant or hotel staff first for non-life-threatening situations — locals know the fastest routes.
